# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Single developer (Winston) plus AI agents operating on their behalf. Used during focused work sessions: creating tickets from ideas, triaging backlogs, updating status mid-task, reviewing what agents have touched. The audience is never a team — it's one person moving fast with intelligent tools.

## Product Purpose

A local-first issue tracker for a developer who works with agents. Originated as a Beads clone. Aspires to be "Linear for one": the same precision and confidence, without the team overhead. Tickets are the interface between human intent and agent execution.

## Brand Personality

Precise, confident, sparse. The product steps back and lets the work be visible.

Reference: Linear. What to take from it: high information density without feeling dense, keyboard-first interactions, restraint in decoration, dark-mode-native feel, typography as the primary design element, progressive disclosure as the governing pattern.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboards: hero metrics, gradient accents, big rounded cards
- Notion-style softness: too friendly, too much whitespace, too document-like
- Jira / project management bloat: visual noise, status badges everywhere, overwhelming hierarchy
- GitHub Issues: functional but unstyled, no sense of craft

## Design Principles

1. **Progressive disclosure.** Show what's needed for the current task. Complexity surfaces on demand, not by default.
2. **Speed is a feature.** Every interaction should feel instant. No loading states where none are needed. No animation that delays.
3. **Typography does the work.** Hierarchy through weight and scale, not color or decoration. The ticket title is the most important element on any screen.
4. **Density without clutter.** High information per pixel, but grouped and breathable. Scannable, not crowded.
5. **Agents are first-class.** The UI must be as legible to an agent reading state as it is to a human. Labels, statuses, and structure should be unambiguous.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as a sensible floor. Keyboard navigation essential (keyboard-first product). Sensible defaults for reduced motion. No accessibility accommodations have been stated beyond this.
